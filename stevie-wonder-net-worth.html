Early Life: A Child Prodigy with a Golden Voice

Stevie Wonder was born Stevland Hardaway Judkins on May 13, 1950, in Saginaw, Michigan.

He was the third of six children born to Calvin Judkins and Lula Mae Hardaway, who divorced when he was four.

He moved to Detroit with his mother and siblings, where he sang in the choir of his local Baptist church.

Stevie also showed an amazing talent for music, learning to play the piano, harmonica, drums, and other instruments by ear.

Wonder was born six weeks premature, which caused him to develop retinopathy of prematurity (ROP), a condition that damaged his retinas and left him blind.

However, this did not stop him from pursuing his passion for music.

He was discovered by Ronnie White, a member of the Motown group The Miracles, who introduced him to Berry Gordy Jr., the founder of Motown Records.

Gordy signed him to his label as Little Stevie Wonder when he was only 11 years old.
